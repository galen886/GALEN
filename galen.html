September - October

SAT prep and disassociation

During this time-period, I was really feeling down I would say. I was really demotivated, unmoved, just was living through life as if it was a spongebob re-run for the thousandth time. At that time, it was my last year in high school, and I was studying for the SAT, but I was more focused on everything but the SAT itself. I found myself doing anything but studying for the SAT, and I even had some online tutor, but I didn’t like him at all. Not because of the SAT, rather, his teaching methods are just not teaching and it is almost impossible cooperating with someone like that who doesn’t see any flaws in what they do. But nevertheless, I signed myself up for the SAT in October, and I instilled fake confidence in myself that I was going to pass that test, even making myself feel hype waking up at 4am and watching some videos of how to use desmos the day of the test. The feeling I had when taking that SAT test in October was guilt. I sat in that car and told my mom ‘I passed that test’, but when 2 weeks came and I was at home, I knew I couldn’t dodge the inevitable. Prior to me coming home, I knew my score already since I got the email, but I didn’t know when it was going to get to my parents. So as I stared long at the dense, but cracking ceiling in my AP Literature class, I was just imagining the result of the conversation I am going to have when I get home. I knew it was going to be something I probably haven’t seen in a while, since I haven’t failed or found myself into trouble for a very long time leading up to this point, so the thought of it created tension in my mind as I walked out of the school building.

The same garage that shuts all the time with its loud clump, shut very lightly today, with its thoughts being more happy today than on most days.
